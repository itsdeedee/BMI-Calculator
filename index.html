What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure that uses height and weight to estimate how much body fat someone has. This is determined by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters (kg/mÂ²). BMI (short for "Body Mass Index") is a rating tool to help determine if you are at a healthy weight and what category of underweight, normal weight or overweight/obese that in which you fall.

Limitations of BMI

Age

BMI may not adequately measure nicely bodied fat amongst the elderly as a result of muscle loss and fats redistribution.

Gender

The distribution of fat in men and women may differ, which could affect the results when they calculated BMI.

Muscle

A person who has very high muscle mass may weigh more, giving them a larger BMI despite not having too much body fat.

Pregnancy

Pregnancy involves weight gain and body changes, during pregnancy time BMI cannot be used to decide healthy weight.

Race

Ethnic differences in body composition can result to misinterpretation of BMI outcomes across different races.
